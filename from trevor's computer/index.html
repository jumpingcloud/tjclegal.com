About Trevor:

Trevor Clarke is the owner of The Law Offices of Trevor J. Clarke. Trevor is a member of the inaugural class of the Chicago Bar Foundation's Justice Entrepreneurs Project (JEP), a program that incubates socially conscious solo law practices. Through his involvement with the JEP, Trevor has served as a Resident Attorney at The Law Project, where he counsels clients intent on bettering the community.

Like the clients he serves, Trevor seeks to make a positive impact on the community, and does so in and out of the legal world. As a lawyer, Trevor provides pro-bono representation to borrowers involved in mortgage foreclosure actions and gives advice to low income callers through the CARPLS legal hotline. As a member of the community, Trevor is an active volunteer at The Old Town School of Folk Music, Chicago Public Radio, the Chicago Aikido Club, Organizing Neighborhoods for Equality: Northside, and Working Bikes. At Working Bikes, Trevor is a board member, Corporate Secretary, General Counsel, and a volunteer bicycle mechanic.

Trevor earned his J.D. summa cum laude, Order of the Coif, from the DePaul College of Law. While at DePaul, Trevor gained experience working in a host of legal settings that included a summer associateship and clerkship with a boutique intellectual property law firm, a fellowship with the National Commission Against Biopiracy in Peru, and an externship at the MacArthur Foundation's Office of General Counsel. Prior to pursuing his legal career, Trevor conducted basic science research at the Feinberg Cardiovascular Institute of Northwestern University and taught English in Japan.
